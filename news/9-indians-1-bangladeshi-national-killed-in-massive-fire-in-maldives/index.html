Maldives fire: Officials said 10 bodies were recovered from the upper floor of a building destroyed in the fire, which originated from a ground-floor vehicle repair garage. Firefighters try to douse a fire that broke out in a building lodging foreign workers in Maldives capital Male on November 10, 2022. At least 10 people were killed and several others injured November 10 when a fire swept through cramped lodgings of foreign workers in the Maldives capital Male, the fire service said. (AFP photo) Mali: At least ten people were killed and several others were injured in a fire that broke out in a garage in Male, the Maldivian capital city, early on Thursday, officials said.The fire swept through cramped lodgings of foreign workers in the Maldives capital Male, the fire service said. In a tweet on Thursday morning, the National Disaster Management Authority of Maldives said: “NDMA has established an evacuation centre in Maafannu Stadium for those displaced and affected by the fire in Male. Arrangements are being made to provide relief assistance and support.” It made no mention of the casualties. A security official said the dead included nine Indians and a Bangladesh national.
